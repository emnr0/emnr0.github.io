Project 1: Plastic Brick Making Machine

This is my final year project! Ta-daaaa! A silly little project... It was a mess, but it ended with an A, and my graduation. So, I am grateful for it all the same. After the boring introduction, I'd like to explain what this machine does. This machine is a brick making machine. It takes plastic waste and turns it into plastic bricks. The machine is made up of three components: The hopper, the extruder, and the moulding machine. It is meant to contain all three processes in one single machine! How convenient?! Apparently not very...

Project 2: The Plastic Brick

The design of the plastic brick was a project of its own. The design process can be divided into two parts: The brick design, and the mold design. A lot of considerations were made to ensure that the brick is strong, light, and easy to mold. For the mold, it had to be made of a material that is strong, and easy to machine. The material had to withstand heating and cooling without much expansion or contraction. Ventilation was also a concern, as the plastic needs to cool down quickly to hardenØŒ so the mold included ventilation holes to allow air to flow through it. The mold design also included an ejection mechanism that ejects the brick out of the mold automatically when the brick is ready--heat sensors were added to the inside of the mold to measure that.
